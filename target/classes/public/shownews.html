Dr. Wang Handing deliver a speech

 

 


    Many practical problems in the industry can be modeled as black box optimization
    problems.
    The optimization method requires multiple evaluation of candidate solutions,
    but the actual problem function evaluation is costly or has multiple precisi
    ons,
    which hinders the vertical application of existing optimization methods.
    For example,
    the function evaluation in the design of the aircraft is performed by
    aerodynamic simulation. Usually,
    the simulation requires time in hours, so the optimization method that
    requires thousands of function
    evaluations cannot
    find the optimal solution in a limited time. . The data-driven
    optimization method is an emerging artificial
    intelligence methodology.
    The evolutionary computation is used as the optimization method.
    The actual problem function evaluation is
    regarded as data. The existing
    machine learning algorithm is used to train the approximate
    function evaluationto assist the optimization
    method to search. Improve th
    e practicality of traditional optimization algorithms.

 

     Speaker Profile: Wang Handing, Ph.D.,  Xidian University, is a professor at the Institute of Artificial Intelligence. In 2018, she was selected into the Shanxi Youth Thousand Talents Program. Her research interests include computational intelligence, machine learning, multi-objective optimization, and proxy models. In the past five years, she has published 30 high-level papers, including IEEE Trans. on Evolutionary Computation, IEEE Trans. on Cybernetics, Evolutionary Computation and Information Sciences, and one of them was selected as IEEE. Computational Intelligence Society's current Spotlight article. During the overseas study period, she participated in a UK-based Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Grants Agency (EPSRC) project "Data-driven surrogate-assisted evolutionary fluid dynamic optimization".
Dr. Wang HanDing is currently a very active young scholar in the field of international computational intelligence research. She is currently Chairman of the Task Force 13 of the IEEE Computing Technology Applications Technical Committee of IEEE Computational Intelligence Society. Part-time computing intelligence international journal IEEE Computation Intelligence Magazine and pattern recognition international journal "Complex & Intelligent Systems" editorial (Associate Editor). She has served as guest editor of the internationally renowned journals Neurocomputing, IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computational Intelligence and IEEE Access, and served as the top international conference on evolutionary computing, the Genetic and Evolutionary Computation Conference. Member of the Program Committee of the IEEE Congress of Evolutionary Computation and several other international conferences. She has served as a reviewer for several international top journals in the field of computational intelligence.
